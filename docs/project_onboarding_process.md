# CrackedMilk: Project Onboarding Process
*A repeatable, phase-by-phase checklist to implement for every project we onboard.*

---

## Phase 1: Product Audit — Is This Worth Marketing?
*Do this before spending a single hour on marketing.*

### 1A. Painkiller vs. Vitamin Check
Ask: What happens to the customer if they don't solve this problem?

**Painkiller signals (proceed):**
- [ ] Major negative consequence if unsolved (lost money, legal risk, wasted time)
- [ ] Customers use it daily or weekly
- [ ] Mostly B2B (or high-stakes B2C)
- [ ] Hard to explain to your parents (boring = good)

**Vitamin signals (stop and reposition first):**
- Consequence of not using it = fewer likes, missed habits, minor annoyance
- Mostly B2C, used monthly or less
- Sounds hyped up and exciting

> **Rule:** If it's a Vitamin, find the Painkiller angle before touching any marketing. Do not proceed until this is resolved.

### 1B. Product Viability Check
- [ ] Can a first version be built in under 1 month?
- [ ] Do we have a genuine customer insight competitors are missing?
- [ ] Do we know which single channel will drive 80% of traffic in the first 6 months — and do we know how to use it?
- [ ] Is the founder/team genuinely excited about this for 6+ months?

**Deliverable:** A clear "yes, proceed" or a pivot decision. Document it.

## Phase 2: Strategy — 10 Minutes That Prevent 90% of Mistakes
*Write down answers. Generic answers = you don't understand your audience yet.*

Answer all 7 questions before any execution:

1. **What one specific problem are we solving?**
2. **Which audience segment craves this solution the most?**
3. **What is their current go-to solution (the status quo)?**
4. **What is good and bad about that status quo?**
5. **How is this product undeniably better than the alternatives?**
6. **What pricing is a no-brainer for the audience and still profitable for us?**
7. **How will we get the first 10 customers?**

> If any answer feels generic — stop. That means the audience isn't understood yet. Do customer interviews or use a Value Proposition exercise before continuing.

**Deliverable:** A one-page strategy brief with all 7 answers written down.

---

## Phase 3: Landing Page Fix
*Before driving any traffic, the landing page must convert it.*

- [ ] Rewrite all copy: replace every "we/our" with "you/your"
- [ ] Every heading = **Emotional promise + Rational explanation of how it's delivered**
- [ ] Add social proof (testimonials, founder story) — above the fold
- [ ] Record a Loom-style demo video with founder commentary
- [ ] Write a founder message explaining *why* this product was built
- [ ] Remove all purchase parity pricing and money-back guarantees (attracts low-quality customers)
- [ ] Use only 3–4 colors; no animations; no wide layouts
- [ ] Cap all feature/benefit lists at 3–5 points max
- [ ] Make the next action (CTA) simple and desirable — remove any "subscribe for updates" footer CTAs
- [ ] Remove all superlatives ("fastest," "best," "most popular")

### The Roast Protocol (mandatory before launch)
- [ ] Find 30 people who have never seen the page
- [ ] Watch them use it while sharing their screen — do not help them
- [ ] Document every point of confusion
- [ ] Fix the top 3 issues before launch

**Deliverable:** A landing page that's been through at least one Roast session with documented fixes applied.

---

## Phase 4: First Customers — Validation Before Scale
*Do not run ads. Do not do SEO. Get the first 10 customers manually.*

### Primary tactics (pick 1–2 based on the audience):
- [ ] **Social media where the audience actually hangs out:**
  - Twitter/X → startup founders
  - LinkedIn → B2B / agencies / corporates
  - TikTok / Instagram → B2C
- [ ] **Cold outreach (especially for B2B):** Manual, sincere DMs or emails — no automation, no spam. Offer the product free in exchange for feedback and a testimonial.
- [ ] **Email list (if one exists):** Pre-launch to existing subscribers first.
- [ ] **Product Hunt launch:** Good for early traction and newsletter shoutouts if you hit top 5. Not a long-term strategy.

### Getting feedback from first users:
1. Search social media for people posting about the problem this product solves
2. DM 200 of them — short, genuine message, offer free access
3. Collect feedback; offer free subscription to those who engage
4. Get testimonials from the happiest users → add them to the landing page

**Deliverable:** First 10 customers acquired. Real testimonials collected. Landing page updated with social proof.

## Phase 5: Build the Channel Mix — Sustainable Traffic
*The Product Hunt high is temporary. Build a long-term mix.*

Rank channels by effort and fit for this project. Default priority order:

| Priority | Channel | When to start |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Audience building (social — where audience is) | Week 1 |
| 2 | Cold outreach / cold DMs (B2B) | Week 1 |
| 3 | Free SEO tool (wins search intent + builds links) | Month 1–2 |
| 4 | Programmatic SEO (data-driven pages, not AI spam) | Month 2+ |
| 5 | Weekly newsletter (nurture the traffic you earn) | Month 1+ |
| 6 | Facebook Ads (only after funnel is stable and proven) | Month 3+ |
| 7 | Influencer / affiliate marketing | When budget allows |

**Channels to avoid early:**
- Google Ads (expensive, irrelevant clicks)
- Twitter/X Ads (terrible targeting)
- Newsletter sponsorships (bot-heavy, expensive)
- Reddit audience building (ban risk)
- Referral programs (very hard to make work)

---

## Phase 6: SEO (Start After First Customers)
*SEO takes months. Don't do it before idea validation.*

1. **Find what your audience already searches for** — use keyword research to identify demand
   - Focus: relevant to product, existing demand, low-to-medium competition
2. **Build free tools first** — easiest to rank, easiest to promote, drives backlinks naturally
3. **Write long-tail blog posts** close to purchasing intent (not top-of-funnel "how to" posts)
4. **Build backlinks** by:
   - Launching on product directories for initial Domain Authority
   - Making free tools good enough that media links to them
5. Don't obsess over PageSpeed scores — it's a procrastination trap

---

## Phase 7: Email Marketing Setup
*Build this in parallel with traffic. Every email collected is an asset.*

1. **Lead capture:** Free tool or content that asks for email in exchange for additional value (not a gated paywall — let users try first)
2. **Onboarding sequence (new signups):**
   - Email 1: Freebie / additional value
   - Email 2: Promote paid product (focus on free trial/demo)
3. **Activation sequence (product signups):**
   - Help with onboarding
   - Founder story (why this was built)
   - Testimonials from happy customers
   - Limited discount to re-activate dormant users
4. **Post-purchase sequence:**
   - Thank you + feature request ask
   - Upsell to other products
5. **Weekly newsletter:** Insights + 1–2 curated links + product updates — sent to all emails

---

## Phase 8: Paid Ads (Only When Ready)
*Do not run ads until the funnel is proven.*

**Prerequisites before spending on ads:**
- [ ] Landing page has been through the Roast Protocol
- [ ] At least 10 real customers acquired organically
- [ ] Conversion rate is measured and understood
- [ ] Customer acquisition cost budget is calculated

**When ready:**
- Facebook Ads first (better targeting than Google for most solopreneur products)
- Test every headline and creative — expect to experiment
- Retarget website visitors first before cold audiences

## Weekly Sprint Cadence (Ongoing)
*Every Monday, pick ONE goal. Run the loop all week.*

**The loop:** Audit → Generate → Test → Iterate

**Pick one goal per week from this list:**
- Sharpen positioning
- Talk to customers
- Analyze competitors
- Polish marketing copy
- Build in-demand features
- Get more website visitors
- Post new content
- Build a free tool
- Buy ads
- Send emails
- Get new partnerships
- Increase conversion rate
- Fix a landing page section
- Write email sequences
- Get testimonials
- Experiment with pricing

> **Rule:** If a channel or hook hasn't shown traction in **7 days** — pivot. Don't persist with something that isn't working.

---

## Marketing Copy Quick Reference
*Use this every time copy is written for this project.*

**The formula:**
1. Open with an **emotion / promise** (what the customer *gets*)
2. Follow with a **rational explanation** of *how* the product delivers that promise
3. Mix in stories, testimonials, and specific numbers

**Always avoid:**
- Focusing on features instead of outcomes
- Superlatives: "best," "fastest," "most popular"
- Bullet lists with more than 5 points
- Jargon: "streamline," "supercharge," "leverage synergies"
- Writing about yourself — write about the customer
